Privacy, Confidentiality & Practice Information

Transparency, privacy, and confidentiality are foundational to the therapeutic process

The information below outlines important policies and practices related to this website and clinical care. Please review it carefully and contact me with any questions.

Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important. Information submitted through this website is used only to respond to inquiries and provide information about services. This website may utilize standard technologies such as cookies and analytics to improve performance and user experience.

If you have questions about how information is collected or used, please contact the practice directly.

Website Disclaimer

The information provided on this website is for educational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional mental health care.

Viewing this website or contacting the practice does not establish a therapist-client relationship. If you are experiencing a mental health emergency, please call 911, contact the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline by calling or texting 988, or seek immediate emergency care.

Notice of Privacy Practices

Protecting the privacy of your personal health information is an important part of clinical care. A detailed Notice of Privacy Practices is provided to all clients at the start of treatment and is available upon request.

Please note that standard email is not an encrypted or HIPAA-secure channel. Any communication submitted via email or the contact form on this website should be limited to general scheduling inquiries. For clinical matters or sensitive information, please call directly.

Good Faith Estimate Notice

Under the No Surprises Act, individuals who are uninsured or choose not to use insurance have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate of expected healthcare costs.

You have the right to receive a written estimate of the anticipated cost of services before beginning treatment or upon request. If you have questions about your Good Faith Estimate or your rights under this law, please contact the practice directly.

For additional information about your rights under the No Surprises Act, visit cms.gov/nosurprises.
