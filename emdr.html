What is EMDR Therapy?

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is a research-supported therapy that helps the brain and nervous system heal from trauma and distressing life experiences. By working directly with how memories are stored, EMDR may reduce the emotional and physical impact of past experiences and support deeper healing and integration.

Our work begins by establishing trust and safety and providing practical tools to help you feel grounded and connected throughout the process. Using guided eye movements or gentle bilateral stimulation, EMDR helps the brain reprocess experiences that continue to affect your wellbeing. Many clients find that EMDR facilitates profound healing and meaningful change without requiring repeated retelling of painful events.

EMDR is widely recognized for the treatment of PTSD and complex trauma and may also be beneficial for anxiety, burnout, and other trauma-related concerns, with the goal of supporting integration so that past experiences feel less present and intrusive.

No PTSD Diagnosis Needed

EMDR can be effective for a wide range of difficult life experiences — you do not need a formal PTSD diagnosis to benefit.

EMDR vs. Traditional Talk Therapy

Talk Therapy

Talk therapy often focuses on understanding patterns, developing new perspectives, and building insight through conversation. It is a valuable approach for many concerns and can help you make sense of your experiences over time.

EMDR Therapy

EMDR works more directly with how distressing experiences are stored in the brain and nervous system, helping reduce their emotional impact and promote healing. Many clients experience meaningful change without needing to recount painful events in detail repeatedly.

What to Expect in EMDR Therapy

Every client's EMDR experience is unique and tailored to their individual needs, history, and goals. In general, our work will move through several key phases:

1

History & Treatment Planning

We begin by getting to know one another, exploring what brings you to therapy, and creating a personalized treatment plan that reflects your goals and readiness.

2

Preparation & Stabilization

Before any trauma processing begins, we establish a foundation of safety and stability. I will provide practical tools to help you feel grounded and regulated throughout the process.

3

Reprocessing

Using guided eye movements or bilateral stimulation, we work to reprocess distressing memories and experiences so they become less emotionally charged and intrusive.

4

Integration & Closure

We work toward integration — helping you move forward with greater clarity, resilience, and a renewed sense of safety and self-trust.

EMDR via Telehealth

Many clients are surprised to learn that EMDR can be highly effective through telehealth. When clinically appropriate, bilateral stimulation can be adapted for virtual sessions using visual, auditory, or tactile methods.

As with all trauma-focused work, treatment begins by establishing safety, stability, and coping resources before moving into deeper processing. Your comfort, pacing, and readiness remain central throughout the process.
